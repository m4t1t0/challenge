# Short, present-tense title of the decision

## Context

What is the situation that forces a decision? Describe the constraints, the
forces in tension, and any earlier ADRs this builds on. State the problem
plainly enough that a reader who arrives years later understands *why this was
even a question*. Do not describe the solution here.

## Decision

The decision, stated in one or two sentences as an active choice ("We do X").
Follow with the specifics: what exactly changes, where it lives, how it behaves.
Be concrete enough that the code can be checked against it.

## Considered options

- **Chosen option.** One line on why it wins.
- **Alternative A.** Why it was rejected.
- **Alternative B.** Why it was rejected.

## Consequences

What becomes easier, and what becomes harder or is now owed. Include the costs
we knowingly accepted, anything deferred to "later/at scale", and the seams this
decision leaves for future evolution.
